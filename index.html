What you can do

Do Not Recycle Or Trash CFL Bulbs

If CFL light bulbs are recycled or trashed, mercury will seep into the environment, contaminate the air and water, and result in higher chances of mercury poisoning.

What you can do

Find Mercury Recyclers Near You

Mercury Recyclers are prepared to handle CFL light bulbs in the best way possible, making sure the horrible effects of mercury are not allowed to flourish.

What you can do

Dispose Of Properly When Broken

If a CFL breaks, it is important to dispose of it properly. Make sure everyone leaves the room, then air out the room before recycling the glass shards in a CFL recycling center.

What you can do

Follow US EPA Guidelines For Safety

The Environmental Protection Agency has a number of guidelines on how to best recycle CFL bulbs in your location. It also provides details on how to prevent mercury poisoning.
